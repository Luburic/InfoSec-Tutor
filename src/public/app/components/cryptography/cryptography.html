How to define cryptography? One of our favorite definitions is from Handbook of Applied Cryptography by Menezes, van Oorschot and Vanstone that says that cryptography is the study of mathematical techniques related to aspects of information security such as confidentiality, data integrity, entity authentication, and data origin authentication. As such, cryptography is not the only means of providing information security, but rather one set of techniques. So, what are cryptographic goals? Looking at the definition previously given, we can conclude that the information security has a variety of services. And the main goal of cryptography is to cover their theoretical and practical aspects. The services are:

  • Confidentiality - includes rules for limiting access or placing restrictions on the content of information. This could be provided using different techniques which include mathematical algorithms or physical protection.
  • Data integrity - involves different techniques for maintaining the accuracy and consistency of data. This includes detection of unauthorized data manipulation such as insertion, deletion or substitution.
  • Authentication - refers to the process of determining whether someone of something is, in fact, who or what it is declared to be.
  • Data origin authentication - enables the recipient to verify that messages have not been tampered with in transit (data integrity) and that they originate from the expected sender (authenticity).

And Why is cryptography important? For example, every day thousands of people interact over the Internet - they sent e-mails to each other, use the Internet for online business, marketing, research, learning, etc. Cryptography makes these activities safe and secure. Encrypting the data transmitted between the users, allows people to do online banking, or to make online purchases with their credit cards and without worrying any of their account information is being compromised. By encrypting e-mail, we can ensure that it is very difficult to be read by any unwanted third party. Using digital signatures in the important documents can provide us better security in case of any kind of falsification. All of this mean only one thing - cryptography is widely used, and not only over the Internet, but also in phones, televisions , and a variety of other common household items.
