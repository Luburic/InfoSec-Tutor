If you are inspired to deepen your knowledge and understanding of RSA algorithm and underlying mathematics, we encourage you to read some of the following papers:

  1. Rivest R., Shamir A., Adleman L., 1978, A Method for Obtaining Digital Signatures and Public-Key Cryptosystems, Communications of the ACM, 21 (2)
  2. RSA Laboratories, 2012, PKCS#1 v2.2:RSA Cryptography Standard
  3. Boneh D., 1999, Twenty Years of Attacks on the RSA Cryptosystem, Notices of the American Mathematical Society (AMS) Vol. 46, No. 2
  4. Stinson D. R., 2006, Cryptography Theory and practice 3rd edition, Chapman & Hall/CRC
  5. Stallings W., 2005, Cryptography and Network Security Principles and Practices 4th Edition, Prentice Hall
  6. Niven I., Zukerman H. S., 1972, Introduction to the Theory of Numbers, Wiley, New York
  7. Bleichenbacher D., 1998, Chosen ciphertext attacks against protocols based on the RSA encryption standard PKCS #1. In CRYPTO '98, volume 1462 of Lecture Notes in Computer Science, Springer-Verlag
  8. Bellare M., Rogaway P., 1995, Optimal Asymetric Encryption - How to Encrypt with RSA. Advances in Cryptography - Eurocrypt '94. volume 950 of Lecture Notes in Computer Science, Springer Verlag
